Dissertation: Connected matchings in special familes of graphs.

Studied extremal and computational problems of "connected matchings", a graph matching in which no two edges have mutually non-adajcent endpoints. Applications include cloud administration, and the bipartite margin shop problem.

Other research projects

  • Perfomance of Covert Networks

    Studied the properties of graphs modeling networks of covert operatives vulnerable to "subversion". Determines the optimal tree structre for such a network under a performance measure suggested by Doty. (Sildes from 2011 talk at Joint Mathematical Meetings.)

  • Totally Antimagic Graphs

    An antimagic graph labeling is a consecutive {1,2,...,e} edge labeling with the property that the sum of labels incident to each vertex is distinct. This research characterizes totally antimagic graphs, i.e., graphs that admit no consecutive edge labelings that are not antimagic.
